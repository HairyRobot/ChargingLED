Charging LED Help

Battery Charging LED is developed and tested for the Galaxy Nexus, because the phone don't provide any indication during charging. It may work on other phones (NO GUARANTEE).
Once the app is installed and setup, the phone's LED will blink in different colour during charging with screen off.

Battery Level Colour
100% Green
95% - 99% Green (blink)
90% - 94% Blue (blink)
75% - 89% White (blink)
50% - 74% Yellow (blink)
25% - 49% Purple (blink)
Below 25% Red (blink)

Note:
(i) Due to hardware limitation, the colour the LED emit may looks different.
(ii) Not all devices support the same number of colours as the Galaxy Nexus. e.g. Some motorola devices only support one colour (white).
(iii) Not all devices support LED blinking or not-blinking.

Setup

There are 3 steps to setting up this app on the Galaxy Nexus for "LED" charging notification.

(1) Enable the "Monitor charging" option in app.
(2) Enable the "Charging LED" option in app.
(3) Enable the "Pulse notification light" option in "Settings > Display".

Note:
(i) Step (3) may not be available/requires on all devices.
(ii) The app can operate in any combination of "LED", "Notification" and "Sound" charging notification mode.
(iii) On devices without LED (e.g. Nexus 7) or LED not functioning as desired, the app may still be useful, as it offer sound notification.

Using the debug mode

If enabled, a toast message is displayed upon the charging cable is plugged in.

The purpose of this is to verify the "ACTION_POWER_CONNECTED broadcast" is recognized by the system, and the app is notified.
For more information, see Testing below.

Testing

The app is tested on the Galaxy Nexus (GSM), results below:

Works
  • Galaxy Nexus (4.0.4)
  • Galaxy Nexus (4.1.1)
  • Galaxy Nexus (4.2.1)
Don't Works
  • Galaxy Nexus (4.0.1)
  • Emulator (4.0.1)
  • Emulator (4.0.3)
The reason for the failure is because the system failed to recognize the "ACTION_POWER_CONNECTED broadcast" (detect charging cable is plugged in), this is fixed in version 4.0.4.

If this unfortunate event happened with your device, there is nothing I (developer of this app) can do. I had to wait until my Nexus is upgraded to 4.0.4 before I can see it working on a real device. Your best bet is to email your device's manufacturer, and see if they will release a firmware update.

Information that may help for your devices

*** For Nexus 5X users on Android 7 ***

1 - Goto "Settings > System UI Tuner > Other > Power Notification Controls" and turn it on.
2 - Goto "Settings > Notifications > Charging LED" and change the notification level from Auto (tap on the 'A') to Level 4 (one level higher to the right).

Note:
- The above options may also exists on different devices, so take a look yourself.

*** For Android 7.1.1 (Lineage OS) ***

Try set notifications to be at least level 4.
This may also apply to all Android 7. So give it a check.

*** For Samsung Galaxy S3 users ***

The GS3 actually have a built in charging LED indicator option.
The app maybe having conflict with this option, try disable this first. "Setting > LED indicator > Charging".
As I don't have access to a GS3, you will have to test it yourself.

How does this type of app work

I know two methods.

Method 1
(a) System detect charging cable is plugged in.
(b) Start service to blink LED.
(c) Detect charging cable is unplugged.
(d) Stop service, stop LED blinking.

Method 2
(a) Service (running in background) to monitor charging.
(b) Start LED blinking, if charging.
(c) Stop LED blinking, if stop charging.

As method 2 does not rely on system detecting "ACTION_POWER_CONNECTED broadcast" to start a service, it should work on more devices, but it requires a service running constantly in the background.

This app uses method 1, because similar apps I found in Play Store uses method 2, and I don't want a service constantly running in the background, also, all I needs is a charging indicator.
